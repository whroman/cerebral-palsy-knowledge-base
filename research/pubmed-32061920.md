# Pain in adults with cerebral palsy: A systematic review and meta-analysis

[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32061920/)

## Key Findings

> "A meta-analysis revealed a high pain prevalence of 70% (95% CI 62-78) in adults with cerebral palsy"

> "Adults with cerebral palsy should be routinely screened for pain and treated accordingly"

- Sample: 1,243 participants across 15 studies, mean age 34.3 years
- Pain prevalence: 70%
- Women had higher pain rates than men (P\<0.001)
- Pain location: predominantly legs (76%)
- Pain severity: mean 3.7/10
- Pain interference: mean 3.5/10
- Increased odds of pain in GMFCS levels II and IV
