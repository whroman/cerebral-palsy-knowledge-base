# Epidemiology of Cerebral Palsy in Adulthood: A Systematic Review and Meta-analysis of the Most Frequently Studied Outcomes

[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32059945/)

## Key Findings

### Study Scope
- Systematic review and meta-analysis
- Examined most frequently studied outcomes in adults with cerebral palsy

### Population-Level Outcomes

> "On average, adults with cerebral palsy experience fatigue, a majority experience pain and are ambulant, with 40% employed and 30% living independently."

### Key Statistics
- Pain prevalence: majority of adults
- Fatigue: common experience
- Ambulatory status: majority maintain mobility
- Employment rate: approximately 40%
- Independent living: approximately 30%

### Implications
- Adults with cerebral palsy face significant quality of life challenges
- Employment and independent living remain barriers for majority
- Pain and fatigue management are priority concerns
