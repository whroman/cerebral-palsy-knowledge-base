Research Notes

Healthcare Transition

The shift from pediatric to adult healthcare is one of the most challenging periods for people with cerebral palsy. Many experience a sudden loss of coordinated care, leaving them to navigate an unfamiliar system alone.

The Transition Gap​

No standardized transition tool or approach exists across programs.

This lack of structure leads to poor outcomes including:

  • Housing instability
  • Unemployment
  • Difficulty forming relationships
  • Increased hospital admission rates
  • Decreased use of rehabilitation services

Systemic Barriers​

Adults with cerebral palsy face significant obstacles in accessing appropriate care:

Provider availability

  • Limited adult providers accept patients with cerebral palsy
  • Adult physicians often lack training in cerebral palsy management
  • Specialists familiar with childhood cerebral palsy may not see adults

System gaps

  • Poor communication within health service teams
  • Lack of financial resources for specialized care
  • No designated "medical home" for ongoing coordination

Knowledge gaps

  • Adult healthcare providers unfamiliar with cerebral palsy [Citation not found: pmc-5073482]
  • Patients encounter physicians lacking cerebral palsy expertise [Citation not found: pubmed-26754030]

Patient Experience​

Young adults with cerebral palsy often feel unprepared and abandoned by the healthcare system. [Citation not found: pubmed-26754030]

Common experiences during transition:

  • Lack of transition preparedness
  • Difficulty navigating the adult system
  • Gaps in seamless care
  • Limited accessibility to specialists

Young adults "felt that they had been abandoned by the healthcare system and lacked a medical home."

What Helps​

Research identifies protective factors that improve transition outcomes:

Family Involvement​

Continued family support during and after transition provides stability and advocacy.

Self-Efficacy Promotion​

Building independence and self-management skills before transition prepares young adults to navigate adult systems.

Early Introduction to Adult Care​

Meeting adult care teams before the formal transition creates familiarity and reduces anxiety.

Body Awareness​

Developing awareness of one's own body and how to manage symptoms is critical for self-advocacy in adult healthcare. [Citation not found: pubmed-23614354]

Preparing for Transition​

Before Age 18​

  • Begin discussing transition early in adolescence
  • Identify adult providers who accept cerebral palsy patients
  • Build self-advocacy skills
  • Learn to describe your condition and needs
  • Develop body awareness and symptom management strategies [Citation not found: pubmed-23614354]
  • Create a portable medical summary

During Transition​

  • Request a formal transition meeting with pediatric and adult providers
  • Ensure medical records transfer completely
  • Identify a primary care physician as your "medical home"
  • Connect with adult rehabilitation services
  • Build a new care team before losing pediatric coverage

After Transition​

  • Establish regular check-ups with adult providers
  • Maintain relationships with specialists (physiatry, neurology, orthopedics)
  • Monitor for functional changes common in adulthood
  • Stay connected to cerebral palsy community resources

Finding Adult Providers​

Adult cerebral palsy care is often fragmented. Consider:

Physiatrists (PM&R physicians) Many specialize in adult cerebral palsy and can coordinate care across specialists.

Academic medical centers University hospitals often have lifespan programs or adult cerebral palsy clinics.

Cerebral palsy organizations The Cerebral Palsy Research Network and similar organizations maintain provider directories.

Key Points​

  • No standardized transition approach exists—preparation is essential
  • Limited adult providers accept cerebral palsy patients
  • Family involvement and self-efficacy are protective factors
  • Early introduction to adult care teams improves outcomes
  • A primary care "medical home" helps coordinate ongoing care
