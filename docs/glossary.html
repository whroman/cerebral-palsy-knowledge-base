Glossary

Medical and technical terms used throughout this knowledge base.

A​

AAC (Augmentative and Alternative Communication) Devices and methods that supplement or replace speech. Includes communication boards, speech-generating devices, and eye-gaze systems. See Equipment & Devices.

AFO (Ankle-Foot Orthosis) A brace extending from below the knee to the foot that supports ankle positioning and improves gait. See Equipment & Devices.

Aspiration When food, liquid, or saliva enters the airway instead of the esophagus. Common concern with dysphagia. See Secondary Conditions.

Ataxic cerebral palsy Type characterized by problems with balance and coordination rather than stiffness.

Athetoid/Dyskinetic cerebral palsy Type characterized by involuntary, uncontrolled movements.

B​

Baclofen A muscle relaxant that reduces spasticity. Available as oral medication or via intrathecal pump. See Spasticity Management.

Botulinum toxin (Botox) Injectable medication that reduces spasticity in targeted muscles. Effects last 3-6 months. See Spasticity Management.

C​

CFCS (Communication Function Classification System) Five-level system classifying communication ability. See CFCS.

Contracture Permanent shortening of muscles or tendons that limits joint movement. Often results from prolonged spasticity.

D​

Diplegia Cerebral palsy primarily affecting the legs more than the arms.

Dyskinesia Involuntary, uncontrolled movements. See athetoid/dyskinetic cerebral palsy.

Dysphagia Difficulty swallowing. Affects up to 85% of people with cerebral palsy. See Nutrition & Feeding.

E​

Epilepsy Neurological condition causing recurrent seizures. Affects approximately 50% of people with cerebral palsy. See Epilepsy Management, Secondary Conditions.

Equinus Walking on toes due to tight calf muscles. See Gait Patterns.

F​

FEES (Fiberoptic Endoscopic Evaluation of Swallowing) Camera-based swallowing assessment where a scope is passed through the nose. See Nutrition & Feeding.

FIM (Functional Independence Measure) Standardized assessment tool measuring independence in daily activities. See Functional Independence.

G​

Gastrostomy tube (G-tube) Feeding tube placed directly into the stomach through the abdominal wall for long-term nutritional support. See Nutrition & Feeding.

GMFCS (Gross Motor Function Classification System) Five-level system classifying mobility. Level I walks without limitations; Level V transported in wheelchair. See GMFCS.

H​

Hemiplegia Cerebral palsy affecting one side of the body (arm and leg on the same side).

Hypertonia Increased muscle tone (stiffness). Includes spasticity.

I​

ITB (Intrathecal Baclofen) Surgically implanted pump delivering baclofen directly to spinal fluid. See Spasticity Management.

M​

MACS (Manual Ability Classification System) Five-level system classifying hand function. See MACS.

N​

Neurologist Physician specializing in nervous system disorders. Manages seizures and some spasticity medications.

Neuropsychologist Psychologist specializing in brain-behavior relationships. Conducts cognitive and autism assessments. See Cognitive Assessment.

O​

Orthosis (plural: Orthoses) External device supporting body structure. Includes AFOs, hand splints, trunk supports. See Equipment & Devices.

Orthotist Specialist who designs, fabricates, and fits orthoses.

P​

Physiatrist Physician specializing in Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation (PM&R). Key coordinator for cerebral palsy care. See Physiatrist Role.

PM&R (Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation) Medical specialty focused on restoring function. Physiatrists are PM&R physicians.

Q​

Quadriplegia Cerebral palsy affecting all four limbs and usually the trunk.

S​

Scoliosis Abnormal lateral curvature of the spine. Affects 46% of adults with cerebral palsy. See Secondary Conditions.

Spasticity Increased muscle tone causing stiffness and resistance to movement. A core feature of spastic cerebral palsy. See Spasticity Management.

Spastic cerebral palsy Most common type, characterized by stiff, tight muscles.

T​

THA (Total Hip Arthroplasty) Hip replacement surgery. Achieves 87% pain relief and 79% functional improvement in adults with cerebral palsy. See Orthopedics & Hip.

Tizanidine Oral muscle relaxant used for spasticity. See Spasticity Management.

Transition Process of moving from pediatric to adult healthcare. See Transition to Adult Care.

V​

VFSS (Videofluoroscopic Swallowing Study) X-ray video assessment of swallowing, also called modified barium swallow. Gold standard for dysphagia evaluation. See Nutrition & Feeding.
