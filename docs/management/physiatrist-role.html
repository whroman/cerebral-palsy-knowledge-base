The Central Role of Physiatrists in Adult CP Care

What is a Physiatrist?​

A physiatrist is a physician specializing in Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation (PM&R). For adults with cerebral palsy, they serve as the central coordinator of comprehensive care across multiple specialties.

Core Functions in CP Care​

Comprehensive Assessment and Coordination​

"Physiatrists and physical medicine and rehabilitation experts guide patients in comprehensive care including functional issues, spasticity and pain management, and physical therapy exercises to retain and improve mobility, reduce fatigue, and enhance mental health in young adults" 1

"Identifying and addressing the causes of functional decline requires comprehensive and careful assessment. Depending on individual needs, a patient may see a Primary Care doctor, a Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation doctor (PMR), also known as a Physiatrist, a Neurologist, an Orthopedic Surgeon, and doctors specializing in other areas of medicine where needed" 2

Assessment Process​

Initial Evaluation​

"During an appointment, body weight and other measurements are recorded, and the patient's medications are reviewed. A PM&R specialist performs an evaluation and, if necessary, helps coordinate referrals to other service providers" 3

"At your first visit, our physical medicine and rehabilitation specialists will help you figure out what's going on in your body. At your appointments, your provider will talk with you about each of your symptoms and do a physical examination. They might order tests like X-rays or a bone scan" 3

Comprehensive Review Includes:​

  • Medical history and current medications
  • Physical examination of movement patterns
  • Assessment of pain and spasticity
  • Evaluation of functional abilities
  • Screening for secondary conditions

Care Coordination​

Essential Role​

"Having care coordinators on board is absolutely essential. So we have a pediatric and an adult care coordinator, they communicate with each other" 4

This coordination is particularly important for:

  • Transition from pediatric to adult care
  • Managing multiple specialists
  • Ensuring continuity of care
  • Preventing fragmented treatment approaches

Multidisciplinary Team Leadership​

"Working closely with colleagues in cardiology, pulmonology, orthopedics, nutrition, speech-language therapy, and our Wheelchair and Durable Medical Equipment Clinic, we help patients access advanced treatments to optimize their overall health and reduce the impact of changing symptoms on daily life" 3

"Care in the Adult Cerebral Palsy Clinic is overseen by specialists in physical medicine and rehabilitation (PM&R). Since each patient's needs are unique, PM&R specialists can collaborate with any of following specialists as needed" 3

Treatment Planning​

Developing Comprehensive Approaches​

"They can seek help from a PMR or neurologist to discuss treatment options for pain and stiffness, including oral medication, botox injections, baclofen pumps, or rehabilitative therapy" 2

Treatment planning typically addresses:

  • Spasticity management
  • Pain control strategies
  • Mobility preservation and improvement
  • Fatigue management
  • Equipment needs
  • Therapy services coordination

Major Centers with Physiatrist-Led Programs​

Leading Institutions​

  1. UT Southwestern Medical Center

    • "Led by the Chair of the Department of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation who launched a new clinic specifically for adults with cerebral palsy" 1

  2. Cleveland Clinic

    • "Dr. Francois Bethoux, a physiatrist and Chairman of the Department of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation in Cleveland Clinic's Neurological Institute" exemplifies physiatrist leadership 4

  3. University of Michigan

    • "Clinicians in the Department of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation are leading studies about quality of life and health outcomes in teens and adults with cerebral palsy" 3

  4. UCLA Health

    • "Your care team includes orthopedic surgeons, physiatrists, interventional spine care specialists, social workers, physical therapists and neurosurgeons" 5

Specialized Services Coordination​

Areas of Expertise​

Physiatrists coordinate care across multiple domains:

  • Mobility and Function: Gait analysis, mobility device prescription, transfer training
  • Spasticity Management: Medications, botulinum toxin, intrathecal baclofen pumps
  • Pain Management: Comprehensive pain assessment and multimodal treatment
  • Secondary Conditions: Osteoporosis screening, cardiovascular health, fatigue management
  • Equipment Needs: Wheelchair assessment, orthotics, adaptive equipment

Benefits for Adults Never Previously Assessed​

Unique Value for Late Assessment​

For adults with CP who missed childhood interventions, physiatrists offer:

  • Comprehensive baseline assessment
  • Identification of treatable conditions
  • Access to previously unknown resources
  • Coordination of catch-up interventions
  • Development of adult-focused treatment plans

"Taking note of your changing symptoms and diagnosing new problems means you can get treatment sooner" 3

Finding a Physiatrist​

How to Access Services​

  1. Referral Sources:

    • Primary care physician referral
    • Self-referral to PM&R departments at major medical centers
    • Transition from pediatric rehabilitation services

  2. What to Look For:

    • Experience with adult cerebral palsy
    • Access to multidisciplinary team
    • Comprehensive assessment capabilities
    • Care coordination services

  3. Telehealth Options: Many centers now offer initial consultations via telehealth, expanding access for those in remote areas

Key Advantages of Physiatrist-Led Care​

Why This Model Works​

  1. Holistic Perspective: View the whole person, not just individual symptoms
  2. Functional Focus: Emphasis on maximizing independence and quality of life
  3. Coordination Expertise: Trained to manage complex, multi-system conditions
  4. Rehabilitation Mindset: Focus on improvement and adaptation, not just maintenance
  5. Lifespan Approach: Understanding of how CP affects adults differently than children
