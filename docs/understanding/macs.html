Manual Ability Classification System (MACS)

Official Source​

The official website for MACS is www.macs.nu. This is the primary source for information about the Manual Ability Classification System.

Overview​

According to the official MACS website and research publications, "The Manual Ability Classification System (MACS) describes how children with cerebral palsy use their hands to handle objects in daily activities."
It is used for children aged from 4 to 18 years old with cerebral palsy, with a focus on the use of both hands together.

Key Features​

"MACS describes five levels. The levels are based on the children's self-initiated ability to handle objects and their need for assistance or adaptation to perform manual activities in everyday life."

"The classification is designed to reflect the child's typical manual performance, not the child's maximal capacity. It classifies the collaborative use of both hands together."

The Five MACS Levels​

According to the MACS classification:

  • MACS Level I - Objects are handled easily and successfully
  • MACS Level II - Handles most objects but with some reduced quality and/or speed
  • MACS Level III - Handles objects with difficulty – the child will need help to prepare and/or modify activities

(Levels IV and V involve increasingly limited ability to handle objects and greater need for assistance)

Reliability and Validity​

A study published in PubMed demonstrated: "The results demonstrated that MACS has good validity and reliability, with an intraclass correlation coefficient between therapists of 0.97 and between parents and therapist of 0.96, indicating excellent agreement."

Additional Versions​

"A version of the test for children under the age of four years old, the Mini-MACS, was developed in 2016."

The official MACS website provides resources in multiple languages, with recent additions including Vietnamese and Bulgarian translations.

Clinical Use​

The MACS is maintained by the developers and provides downloadable resources, leaflets, and comprehensive information about implementing the MACS system through the official website at www.macs.nu.
